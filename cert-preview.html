** UUC = Unit Under Calibration

Explaination : The UUC reading is averaged from the readings without adjustment, unless UUC before adjustment is reported. The calibration factor (C.F.) is defined as the standard of the quantity that the instrument is intended to measure divided by the UUC reading given by the instrument. To achieve more accuracy, C.F. shall be applied using the equation; result = C.F. x UUC reading.

The conversion coefficient of R to Gy is 8.76 mGy/R (ref. ICRU Report 47 p.23.). The conversion coefficient of Gy to Sv is 1.21 Sv/Gy for Cs-137 and 1.16 Sv/Gy for Co-60 (ref. ISO 4037-3 p.26).

Uncertainties of Calibration : The stated uncertainty is the expanded uncertainty which obtains multiplying of the standard uncertainty and the coverage factor k=2 at approximately 95% confidence. It has been determined in accordance with Guide to the Expression of Uncertainty in Measurement - JCGM 100: 2008.
